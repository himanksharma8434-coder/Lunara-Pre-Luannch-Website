1. Introduction & Scope

Welcome to Lunara ("we," "us," "our," or the "App"). Lunara is a menstrual cycle tracking and wellness companion application developed by Lunara ("Company"), available on Android and iOS platforms.

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use the Lunara mobile application, including all related services, features, and content (collectively, the "Service"). This policy applies to all users worldwide, including those in the European Union (GDPR), the United Kingdom (UK GDPR), the United States — specifically California (CCPA/CPRA), India (DPDP Act 2023), and all other jurisdictions in which we operate.

We recognize that menstrual and reproductive health data is extremely sensitive. We are committed to protecting this data with the highest standards of privacy and security. We do not sell your personal data. We do not share your data with advertisers.

By using Lunara, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please discontinue use of the App immediately.

Contact: lunarahealthtracker@gmail.com

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide Directly

When you register for an account or use certain features, you provide us with the following information:

  • Account information: Name, email address, and password (for email registration); or your Google profile information including name, email, and profile photo (for Google Sign-In); or your phone number (for OTP-based login).
  • Demographic information: Age (minimum 13 years) and gender (Female/Male).
  • Profile data: Profile picture (uploaded via camera or photo library, stored in our cloud storage).
  • Body metrics: Height (cm), weight (kg), as entered by you.
  • Cycle data: Menstrual cycle length, period duration, period start/end dates, and whether your cycle is irregular.
  • Health & wellness logs: Daily symptoms (cramps, bloating, breast tenderness, mood swings, back pain, nausea, etc.), mood states, sleep duration, water intake, basal body temperature (BBT), and other wellness observations you choose to log.
  • Assessment data: Responses to period feeling assessments, sleep assessments, and wellness evaluations.
  • Proxy tracking data: If you indicate you are tracking for someone else (partner, mother, sister, friend, daughter, or other), the tracked person's name and relationship to you.
  • Community content: Posts, comments, and interactions you make in the community feed.
  • Notes: Personal notes you create within the App.
  • Support requests: Feedback messages, bug reports, questions, and the email address you provide when contacting support.
  • Payment information: When subscribing to Lunara Plus, your name, email, and contact number are passed to our payment processor (Razorpay). We do not store your credit card, debit card, UPI, or bank account details on our servers. Payment credentials are handled entirely by Razorpay.

2.2 Information Collected Automatically

  • Device information: Operating system type (Android or iOS) and version, for the purpose of providing platform-appropriate functionality.
  • Error and crash data: Application error logs and crash reports collected via our centralized logging system (LoggerService). These logs may include stack traces, error messages, and the general area of the app where the error occurred. This data is used exclusively for debugging and improving app stability.
  • App usage metadata: Onboarding completion status, last assessment date, notification preferences, theme preference (light/dark mode), and last health data sync timestamp. This data is stored locally on your device and in your cloud profile.

2.3 Information from Third-Party Sources

  • Google Sign-In: If you sign in with Google, we receive your name, email address, and profile photo URL from Google's authentication service.
  • Google Health Connect (Android) / Apple HealthKit (iOS): If you choose to connect your health platform, we read the following data types only with your explicit permission:
    • Steps (daily count)
    • Sleep sessions and duration
    • Heart rate (latest reading)
    • Weight
    • Height
    • Menstruation flow data

We may also write menstruation flow data back to your health platform to keep your records synchronized. This integration is entirely opt-in. You can revoke access at any time from your device Settings.

2.4 Location Data

GPS location: If you use the "Nearby Doctors" feature, we request access to your device's location (fine and coarse). Your coordinates are sent to the OpenStreetMap Overpass API to find nearby hospitals, clinics, doctors, and pharmacies. We do not store your location data on our servers. It is used only for the duration of the search and is not retained.

8. Cookies & Tracking Technologies

We use SharedPreferences and Hive (encrypted local database) for storing your preferences and data locally on your device. flutter_secure_storage is used to securely store authentication tokens. These are standard mobile storage mechanisms, not tracking technologies.

10. Data Security Measures

We implement the following security measures to protect your data:

  • Encryption in transit: All communications between the App and our servers use TLS/HTTPS.
  • Encryption at rest: Data in our Supabase PostgreSQL database is encrypted at rest. Local data on your device is stored in Hive encrypted boxes.
  • Row-Level Security (RLS): Strict RLS policies on our Supabase database ensure that each user can only access their own data.
  • Secure token storage: JWT authentication tokens are stored using flutter_secure_storage (Android Keystore / iOS Keychain).
  • Biometric app lock: Optional biometric authentication (fingerprint/Face ID) via Ghost Mode for additional device-level protection.
  • API key protection: All API keys are stored in environment files excluded from version control (.gitignore).
  • SSL validation: SSL certificate validation is enforced; insecure HTTP overrides are not permitted.
  • No hardcoded secrets: API keys are loaded at runtime from environment configuration, not hardcoded in source code.
  • Account deletion cascade: When you delete your account, all data is permanently removed from our database, including cycle logs, assessments, community content, support tickets, and authentication records.
