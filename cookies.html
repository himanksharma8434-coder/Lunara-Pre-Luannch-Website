1. Do We Use Cookies?

No. Lunara is built with privacy at its core. We do not use cookies, web beacons, tracking pixels, or other browser-based tracking technologies to collect, store, or monitor your personal or online behavior.

Because the Lunara Service is delivered primarily through native mobile applications (available on Android and iOS), traditional web cookies are not used. Our pre-launch landing website is static and does not drop any third-party tracking or advertising cookies onto your computer or browser.

2. Local Device Storage

Instead of cookies, the Lunara mobile application uses standard local device storage mechanisms to save your settings, preferences, and logged cycle data. These files are stored directly on your physical device and are essential to ensure the app works smoothly, even when offline.

Specifically, the App uses the following technologies:

  • SharedPreferences: Used on Android devices to store minor settings like user display preferences, app theme preference (light/dark mode), onboarding completion status, and local notification setup.
  • Hive Database: An encrypted local database box stored on your device that holds your daily health logs, symptom logs, basal body temperature, height, weight, and cycle records. This data is encrypted using native device cryptography.
  • Secure Token Storage (flutter_secure_storage): Used to securely store authentication tokens (using Android Keystore and iOS Keychain Services). This ensures you stay signed in securely without having to enter your credentials every time you open the App.

Note: These storage systems are local to your physical mobile device and do not follow you across the web, nor can they be read by other websites or applications.

3. Third-Party Services

While the website and mobile app do not use cookies, some third-party integrations and payment handlers may require standard session storage to complete actions. For example:

  • Razorpay: If you subscribe to Lunara Plus, payment operations are securely handled through Razorpay. Razorpay may use cookies, tokens, or temporary storage strictly necessary to authenticate your payment and prevent fraud.
  • Google Sign-In: If you use social sign-in, Google uses its own secure tokens and authentication cookies within the system's web frame to log you into your account safely.

4. How to Manage Local Storage

If you wish to clear or disable the local storage files created by the Lunara application, you can do so easily:

  • Log out: Logging out of the Lunara App automatically clears your local secure tokens and sync preferences from your device.
  • Clear App Data: You can wipe all local storage (including Hive database boxes and SharedPreferences) by going to your mobile device's system settings (Settings > Apps > Lunara > Storage > Clear Storage/Data). Warning: Clearing all app data will delete local logs that have not yet been synced to the cloud.
  • Uninstall: Uninstalling the Lunara application from your device completely deletes all local storage and secure keys associated with the app.
